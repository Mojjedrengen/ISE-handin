\section{Reflect on one main point you learned in Sprints 1–2. This must be stated as an educational observation related to the use of Scrum or other work practices (e.g., what did you learn, how). Your reflection must include at least one concrete reference to something that happened in your team (e.g., a backlog item, a PR or commit, a sprint goal, or a note from a retrospective or TA/coach feedback).}
\label{sec:actual}
We learned that or I learned that to many compact meetings leads to waste of time or a feeling that you do not contribute.
In sprint 1 we had our daily sprints on Tuesdays, Wednesdays and Thursdays were it was hard for me to work do to other priorities, so I often had nothing to contribute.
There were also a lot of others from the group who also did not have anything they had done between. 
We came to the conclusion that the problem was that the meetings was not spread out during the week.
So people did not have time to work on the project.
In the next sprint, sprint 2, we decided to spread out meetings so we would have one on Mondays, Wednesdays and Friday.
This is so we have more time to actually do something in between the meetings, since we have other things to do.
