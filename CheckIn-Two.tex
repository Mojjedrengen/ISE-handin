\section{Reflect on one main point you learned in Sprints 3–4. This must be stated as an educational observation related to Scrum or other work methods (e.g., what did you learn, how). Include at least one reference to something concrete from your team’s work during these sprints (e.g., a backlog item, PR, sprint goal, retro note, or interaction with the PO/SM).}
\label{sec:actual}
I have learned to appreciate scrum. Since we have moved to having daily scrum every 2 days, instead of them being bundled together in the middle, we have much more productive meetings.
We have found out that we keep forgetting doing reviews of out pull requests, so we have decided to add another field on our kanban board called "In Review". This was also don due to us keeping ending our sprints with items that had open pull request but not merged into main. This meant that they were not considered done. With this we hope to add some more transparency in out work and help us remember to review pull requests. We had at on point a pull request which had been open for 14 days, spanning 3 sprints. We hope that this does not happen again.
